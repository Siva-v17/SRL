/ 02 — Engagement flow

How a partnership actually runs.

STEP 01

Scoping Call

A working session with the relevant division head to define the question and the proof you actually need.

STEP 02

Proposal & SOW

Fixed-scope or retainer pricing, a pre-registered protocol, and a realistic timeline — no surprise change orders.

STEP 03

Embedded Research

Weekly checkpoints with raw data access through your partner portal — never a black box until the final report.

STEP 04

Report & Hand-off

A full report, dataset and — if you choose to publish jointly — co-authorship terms agreed up front.

/ 04 — Before you ask

Frequently filed questions.

Who owns the data and IP from a commissioned study?

By default, the commissioning partner owns the resulting IP; Stackly retains the right to publish anonymized methodology and negative results unless the SOW states otherwise.

Can a study stay confidential?

Yes — most contract work is under NDA. Our open-data principle applies to our own internal research, not client-commissioned studies.

Do you work with early-stage startups, or only enterprise partners?

About a third of our active engagements are with seed-to-Series-B startups. We offer reduced-rate instrument access for teams under 15 people.

What happens if the result disproves our hypothesis?

You still get a full report. A disproven hypothesis is often the most valuable outcome of a study — it tells you where not to spend the next round of funding.
